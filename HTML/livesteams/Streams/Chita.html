The cheetah, (Acinonyx jubatus), one of the world’s most-recognizable cats, known especially for its speed.
Cheetahs are covered almost entirely with small black spots on a background of pale yellow and have a white underbelly.
Their faces are distinguished by prominent black lines that curve from the inner corner of each eye to the outer corners of the mouth, like a well-worn trail of inky tears.
Cheetahs have a long, slender body measuring 1.2 metres (4 feet), with a long tail (65–85 cm [2–3 feet]) that generally ends in a white tuft.
A cheetah eats a variety of small animals, including game birds, rabbits, small antelopes.
